2. Interpretable Linear Regression Model
    Interpretability is the degree to which a human can consistently predict the model's result. 
    The higher the interpretability of a machine learning model, the easier it is for someone to comprehend why certain decisions or predictions have been made.
    As the linear regression model forces the prediction to be a linear combination of features, the biases and the correction are easily understandable to humans resulting in it  being a interpretable model. In short, linearity leads to the interpretable model.
